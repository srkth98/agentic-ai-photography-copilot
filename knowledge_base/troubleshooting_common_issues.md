# Troubleshooting Common Issues Guide

## Purpose

This guide addresses frequently reported camera problems: blurry photos, excessive
noise, autofocus failures, and rapid battery drain.

## Issue 1: Blurry or Soft Photos

### Symptoms

* Images appear soft, smeared, or "double-exposed"
* Sharpness varies between shots of the same subject/settings

### Possible Causes & Resolution

1. **Camera shake** — shutter speed too slow for handheld shooting. Use a shutter
   speed of at least 1/60s (faster for telephoto lenses, per the "1/focal length"
   rule), enable image stabilization, or use a tripod.
2. **Missed focus** — autofocus locked onto the wrong subject or focus point. Use
   single-point AF and confirm focus before shooting; review images zoomed in to
   check focus accuracy.
3. **Subject motion** — moving subjects blur at slow shutter speeds. Increase
   shutter speed to 1/500s or faster for fast-moving subjects.
4. **Front/back focus (lens calibration)** — if focus consistently lands in front
   of or behind the intended subject across many shots, the lens may need AF
   micro-adjustment (see lens guide).
5. **Dirty or scratched lens/filter** — clean the front element and remove any
   damaged protective filter (see maintenance guide).

## Issue 2: Excessive Noise / Grain

### Symptoms

* Images shot at moderate-to-high ISO show visible speckling, especially in
  shadow areas

### Resolution

1. Lower the ISO when possible — use a wider aperture or slower shutter speed
   (within motion-blur limits) to compensate.
2. Expose correctly in-camera — underexposed images that are brightened in
   post-processing show significantly more noise than correctly exposed images.
3. Apply noise reduction during post-processing (see post-processing guide),
   balancing detail retention with noise smoothing.
4. Shoot RAW for more noise-reduction flexibility than JPEG.
5. If noise appears at low ISO (100-400) or as colored blotches/hot pixels in a
   consistent pattern, this may indicate a sensor issue.

## Issue 3: Autofocus Fails to Lock or "Hunts"

### Symptoms

* Camera repeatedly racks focus back and forth without locking
* Autofocus is slow or unreliable in certain conditions

### Resolution

1. Low light or low-contrast subjects are harder for autofocus systems — try
   focusing on a higher-contrast edge near your subject, or switch to manual
   focus.
2. Ensure the lens's focus mode switch is set to AF (not MF).
3. Clean the lens contacts and rear element — dirt can interfere with AF motor
   communication.
4. Update camera and lens firmware, as AF performance improvements are common in
   firmware updates.
5. If AF hunting happens consistently with one specific lens but not others on the
   same body, the issue is likely the lens's AF motor — consider professional
   repair.

## Issue 4: Battery Drains Quickly

### Symptoms

* Battery percentage drops much faster than expected, especially in cold weather
  or with video/Wi-Fi use

### Resolution

1. Cold temperatures reduce battery capacity — keep spare batteries warm (inner
   jacket pocket) and swap as needed.
2. Disable Wi-Fi/Bluetooth, GPS, and image stabilization preview when not needed,
   as these consume significant power.
3. Reduce screen brightness and the "auto review" duration after each shot.
4. Batteries degrade over time — if a battery that once lasted hundreds of shots
   now drains in dozens, it may need replacement.
5. Use genuine or reputable third-party batteries — some cheap third-party
   batteries have lower true capacity than advertised or communicate inaccurate
   charge levels.

## Escalation Criteria

* If blur, noise, or AF issues persist after ruling out the causes above across
  multiple lenses and settings, the camera body itself may need inspection by an
  authorized service center.
* If a battery shows physical swelling, excessive heat, or a burning smell, stop
  using it immediately and dispose of it safely per local battery recycling
  guidelines — do not continue charging or using a damaged battery.
