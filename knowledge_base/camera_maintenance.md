# Camera Maintenance Guide

## Purpose

This guide covers routine care for camera bodies and lenses, sensor cleaning, and
how to handle equipment in challenging environments.

## Routine Cleaning

### Lens Care

1. Remove dust with a blower (air bulb) before touching the glass with anything.
2. Use a soft lens brush to remove remaining particles.
3. For smudges/fingerprints, apply a small amount of lens cleaning solution to a
   microfiber cloth (never directly on the lens) and wipe in gentle circular
   motions from center to edge.
4. Always keep a UV or clear protective filter on the front element if shooting in
   dusty, sandy, or salty environments — it's cheaper to replace than the lens.

### Camera Body Care

* Wipe the body with a dry or slightly damp microfiber cloth.
* Use a blower to clear dust from buttons, dials, and the battery/card
  compartments.
* Keep rubber grips clean and dry to prevent degradation.

## Sensor Cleaning

### Symptoms of a Dirty Sensor

* Dark spots or smudges that appear in the same position on every photo,
  especially visible at narrow apertures (f/11-f/22) and against plain
  backgrounds (sky).

### Resolution

1. Use the camera's built-in sensor cleaning function (usually in the setup menu)
   — this vibrates the sensor to shake off loose dust.
2. If spots persist, use a manual sensor cleaning kit: a blower for loose dust, and
   sensor swabs with cleaning solution for stuck particles.
3. Always clean in a clean, low-dust environment, with the camera powered by a
   full battery (so the shutter doesn't close mid-clean) and using the camera's
   sensor-cleaning/mirror-lock mode.
4. If you're not comfortable cleaning the sensor yourself, professional sensor
   cleaning services are inexpensive and low-risk.

## Weather and Environmental Care

* **Cold weather**: Batteries drain faster in cold temperatures — carry spares
  and keep them in an inner pocket close to your body. Allow the camera to warm up
  gradually when moving from cold to warm environments to avoid condensation.
* **Humid/tropical conditions**: Store gear with silica gel packets to prevent
  fungus growth on lens elements. Avoid leaving gear in sealed bags in direct sun.
* **Dusty/sandy conditions**: Avoid changing lenses in the open; use a dust cover
  or change lenses inside a bag.
* **Rain**: Use a rain cover or plastic bag with a hole for the lens. Dry the
  camera thoroughly afterward, including battery and card doors.

## Storage

* Store cameras and lenses in a dry cabinet or with silica gel, away from direct
  sunlight and extreme temperatures.
* Remove batteries during long-term storage (months) to prevent corrosion from
  leakage.
* Periodically power on and use equipment that's in long-term storage to keep
  mechanical parts (shutter, aperture blades) functioning smoothly.

## Common Issue: Spots Appearing in Photos

Symptoms:

* Consistent dark or blurry spots in the same location across multiple images

Resolution:

1. Run the sensor cleaning function in the camera menu.
2. Take a test shot of a plain white surface at f/16-f/22 to confirm whether spots
   are on the sensor (consistent across lenses) or on the lens (changes or
   disappears with a different lens).
3. If spots are on the lens, clean the rear and front elements per the lens care
   steps above.
4. If spots persist after sensor cleaning, perform a manual sensor clean or send
   to a professional service.

## Escalation Criteria

* If sensor cleaning does not remove spots after multiple attempts, or if you
  notice scratches on the sensor surface, stop attempting DIY cleaning and send the
  camera to an authorized service center.
* If the camera has been exposed to significant moisture (dropped in water,
  heavy rain exposure) and shows malfunction, power it off immediately, remove the
  battery, and seek professional repair — do not attempt to power on until dried
  and inspected.
