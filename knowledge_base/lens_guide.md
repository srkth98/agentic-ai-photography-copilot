# Lens Selection Guide

## Purpose

This guide helps photographers choose the right lens for their camera system and
shooting style, and troubleshoot common lens-related issues.

## Lens Categories

### Prime Lenses

Fixed focal length (e.g. 35mm, 50mm, 85mm).

* Pros: Wider maximum apertures (f/1.4-f/1.8), sharper images, lighter and cheaper.
* Cons: No zoom flexibility — you must move to reframe.
* Best for: Portraits (85mm), street photography (35mm), low light.

### Zoom Lenses

Variable focal length (e.g. 24-70mm, 70-200mm).

* Pros: Flexible framing without changing lenses, fewer lens swaps.
* Cons: Heavier, often narrower maximum aperture (f/2.8-f/4).
* Best for: Travel, events, wildlife (telephoto zooms).

## Focal Length Quick Reference

| Focal Length | Typical Use |
|---|---|
| 14-24mm (ultra-wide) | Landscapes, architecture, astrophotography |
| 24-35mm (wide) | Environmental portraits, travel, street |
| 35-50mm (standard) | Everyday, street, documentary |
| 50-85mm (short tele) | Portraits |
| 100-300mm (tele) | Sports, wildlife |
| 300mm+ (super tele) | Bird/wildlife photography from a distance |

## Choosing a Lens by Genre

* **Portraits**: 50mm or 85mm prime, wide aperture (f/1.4-f/2.8) for background blur.
* **Landscapes**: 14-24mm or 16-35mm wide-angle zoom, narrow aperture (f/8-f/11).
* **Wildlife**: 100-400mm or 150-600mm telephoto zoom, fast autofocus.
* **Macro**: Dedicated macro lens (e.g. 90-105mm macro) for 1:1 magnification.
* **Astro**: Wide-angle prime with very wide aperture (f/1.4-f/2.8).

## Common Lens Issues

### Issue 1: Lens Not Detected / "No Lens Attached" Error

Symptoms:

* Camera displays "no lens attached" even with a lens mounted
* Autofocus and metering do not work

Resolution:

1. Remove and re-seat the lens, ensuring the mount aligns and clicks into place.
2. Clean the electronic contacts on both the lens and camera body with a dry,
   lint-free cloth.
3. Update camera firmware — older firmware sometimes fails to recognize newer
   lenses.
4. Test the lens on another compatible body (or test another lens on this body)
   to isolate whether the issue is the lens or the camera.

### Issue 2: Soft or Out-of-Focus Images at Wide Apertures

Symptoms:

* Images shot at f/1.4-f/2.8 look soft, especially at the edges
* Focus appears to land slightly in front of or behind the intended subject

Resolution:

1. This may be normal lens softness wide open — try stopping down to f/2.8-f/4 and
   compare sharpness.
2. If focus consistently lands in front of or behind the subject ("front/back
   focus"), use the camera's AF micro-adjustment / lens calibration feature if
   available.
3. Ensure you're using single-point autofocus on the intended subject, not a wide
   focus area that may grab the background.

## Escalation Criteria

* If a lens shows visible internal element damage, fungus, or unusual rattling
  when shaken, send it for professional repair.
* If AF micro-adjustment cannot correct consistent front/back focus across
  multiple distances, the lens or body may need factory calibration.
